We partner with content publishers to create and promote branded versions of the WebMynd search sidebar.

Publishers earn revenue from relevant search advertising delivered by the sidebar add-on that they distribute.

Email us if you'd like to discuss a partnership.
As an online publisher, you know that visitors to your site often dislike the non-contextual display ads that are drawn from your remnant inventory. But you need to make money from your content, and there are few alternatives available.
However, users like, or at least tolerate, relevant search ads on Google. And Google makes piles of cash from those types of ads.
What if you could swap your remnant ad inventory for a cut of the search advertising revenue that your readers are already generating for Google?
This is what WebMynd can do.
how it works
You promote a search sidebar browser add-on on your site. The add-on offers something of value to your audience — personalized searches enhanced with your content — and the install process takes less than ten seconds.
Once the user installs the add-on, the sidebar appears on all their searches. Within the sidebar we show relevant search ads supplied by Yahoo. When a user clicks on those ads, you get the revenue.
The result is that you've effectively swapped your remnant display ad impressions for ongoing revenue from relevant search advertising.
Our current publisher partners include sites like MySpace, Digg, and Clipta. If you're interested in joining them, let us know.
